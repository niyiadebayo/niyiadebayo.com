Why Venture Scale?

When you take $100k from an angel at a $2M valuation, you are committing to a 10x return. That means a $20M business. Depending on your industry, that is $20M in sales. $20M is achievable in most businesses — but will $100k get you there?

Then you raise another round. And another. Valuations soar. You get diluted further with each one. Until even if you hit the goal, the equity upside goes to someone else.

Does it have to be venture scale?

A short story. Growing up, I wanted to be the best footballer. I would sneak out of the house to watch township games at Okere School where my mum worked. I would picture myself as the guy pulling off the dribbles. I wanted bow legs so I could dribble better.

I did not become the best footballer. Messi is. I did not have the years of training and could not make up for it by wishing.

But something changed when I changed the frame. I stopped thinking about being the best footballer and started thinking about our neighborhood league. I could become the best striker there. The level playing field was better — everyone had to go to school, so the practice advantage was less pronounced. We were all fiercely competitive. I started observing the goalkeeper and our best striker. Found weak spots. Amateur keepers are slower on their left side if they are right-handed. By understanding what was actually possible at the level I was playing, I became a decent player fast.

The parallel is direct. Have you considered that if you thought of your venture as something that has to bring sustainable returns rather than outsize upside, you would be more energized to pursue it and have better chances of achieving the goal without destroying yourself?

Can a better strategy be: I want to build a profitable business in a year? Raise little at generous terms. Remember the Bezos story — roughly 20% of the company to angel investors at terms that looked generous at the time. At the earliest stage, it is no better than a gamble. But you can improve the odds by understanding the game you are playing.

If you are playing the valuations game, you must be in a field with a realistic path to $50M+ scale. If you are not, maybe you should be in the small business game — bet on yourself with $20k, build something profitable, and be the captain of your own ship.
