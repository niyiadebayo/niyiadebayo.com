Luck Surface Area

I spent the first two years of my undergraduate education in a Nigerian university. During that time I was determined to secure as many scholarships as possible. Not for the money alone — for the network. I wanted access to people who were also trying to get out, move up, build something. In 2008, five of us in my circle received scholarships from organizations like Agip and Shell.

In 2013, I relocated from Kazan to Moscow. Ten people from my network made the same move to MIT that year. One of those connections referred me for a job at Yandex several years later. That referral became the path to everything I do now.

People call this luck. I think luck has a surface area and you can expand it deliberately.

Place. I moved from a Nigerian university to Kazan, from Kazan to Moscow, from Moscow to MIT. Each move changed what was possible. Not because the new city was better in the abstract — but because it put me in proximity to different opportunities and different people. Sometimes expanding your luck surface area is as simple as changing your city. Sometimes it is as small as changing your neighborhood.

People. The five scholarship recipients in 2008 became a network. That network produced the ten who went to MIT. That group produced the Yandex referral. None of this was orchestrated. But it was cultivated. I spent time with people who were building, applying, pushing. The compound effect of that over a decade is not luck. It is positioning.

Awareness. You have to know what you are looking for. Scholarships existed before I started applying. Moscow existed before I moved there. The opportunities were always available. What changed was that I was paying attention and had positioned myself to act when they appeared.

Nigeria to Kazan to Moscow to MIT to Yandex. Each step made the next one possible. None of them were guaranteed. All of them were prepared for.
