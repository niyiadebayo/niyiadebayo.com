Building Resilience

I got a full ride scholarship for a PhD at the University of Calgary. I had my entire life planned outward from that moment. Build competence, join academia, become Dr. Adebayo. Maybe Prof. Adebayo.

The Canadian embassy said no. Four times.

This was not a guy who had never traveled. By that point I had been to 15+ countries including the US. Never denied a visa before. Four consecutive rejections to the same country for a funded program. I was gutted.

That same summer, I accepted an offer from Skoltech. A few months later, I was at MIT.

The world's best engineering school was never on the original plan. It only became possible because the original plan failed. Calgary would have been fine. MIT was different.

I do not have a lesson to package from this. The story is the lesson. Sometimes the rejection is the routing mechanism.
