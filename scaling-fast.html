Scaling Fast with Marketplaces

We launched Yango in Lusaka. Four things I learned about building density in a transactional marketplace.

1. Identify the constrained side. In every marketplace, one side is harder to attract. In transport, that is drivers. A rider downloads an app in seconds. A driver needs a vehicle, documentation, onboarding. The constraint is always on the side that requires a physical asset or a higher commitment to participate.

2. Saturate the constrained side before launch. We had dozens of scouts registering drivers and getting them road-ready before we opened to riders. If you launch with thin supply, every early rider has a bad experience. First impressions in a new city are not recoverable.

3. Shock the demand side to build the first cohort. Heavy discounts — 90% off trips — to flood initial demand. This gets you your first users fast. But the stickiness of subsidy-driven demand is low. You are borrowing attention, not earning it. Use it to build volume, then move quickly to step 4.

4. Tighten quality from day one. Platform efficiency and quality control cannot wait until you have scale. They are what convert subsidy-driven riders into organic ones. A rider who gets a clean car, a polite driver, and a fair price on the second trip does not need a discount for the third.

The sequence matters. Supply first, demand second, quality always. Get the order wrong and you either launch into an empty marketplace or build a subsidized business that cannot sustain itself once the discounts stop.
